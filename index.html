Introduction to chess:

Chess is a classic board game played by two players on an 8x8 grid board. Each player starts with 16 pieces: a king, a queen, two rooks, two knights, two bishops, and eight pawns. The objective is to checkmate the opponent's king, meaning the king is in a position to be captured and cannot escape. Chess requires strategic thinking, foresight, and tactical prowess as players maneuver their pieces to control the board and outwit their opponent. It's a timeless game of skill and strategy that has been enjoyed for centuries across cultures worldwide.

Simple checker moves:

Checkers, also known as Draughts in some countries, is a classic board game played on an 8x8 grid board. Each player starts with 12 pieces, typically represented by discs of contrasting colors, usually black and white. The objective is to capture all of the opponent's pieces or block them from making any legal moves. Pieces can move diagonally forward, capturing opponent's pieces by jumping over them. If a piece reaches the opponent's back row, it becomes a "king" and gains the ability to move and capture diagonally backwards. Checkers is a game of strategy, foresight, and tactical maneuvering, offering simple rules but deep gameplay that has entertained players of all ages for generations.

Poker is a popular card game that combines elements of skill, strategy, and gambling. It is typically played with a standard 52-card deck, though variations exist. The objective of poker varies depending on the variant being played, but generally involves players betting on the strength of their hand compared to others, with the goal of either having the best hand at the showdown or convincing others to fold their hands.While the earliest known form of the game was played with just 20 cards, today it is usually played with a standard deck, although in countries where short packs are common, it may be played with 32, 40 or 48 cards.
